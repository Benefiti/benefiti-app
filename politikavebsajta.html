Which Technologies do we use?


  1. About cookies

    2. Cookies are small text documents (files) that contain a set of data needed to identify the internet browser of an individual visitor (a string of letters and numbers), which our website sends to the visitor's browser during a visit to the website, and which is stored on the hard drive of the visitor's computer, tablet, or mobile device (Device). Examples of cookies include login credentials, items in your online shopping cart, and tracking identifiers. A cookie on your device stores a small amount of data about your visit to the website. Upon each return visit to the website, the website retrieves the data contained in the cookies in the visitor's browser.

    Cookies can create personal data, i.e., data that relates to an identifiable natural person, either directly or indirectly. Cookies differ by duration, purpose, and the site that generated them. Cookies are set by both our web server and third-party web servers on our website, and they can read, update, or delete them each time you visit the website, depending on the type of cookie. Our website uses temporary and "permanent" cookies.

    Cookies that relate only to one user session (a one-time visit to our website) are temporary cookies, which have a duration limited to your visit to the website. They are automatically deleted when you close your internet browser and are usually considered necessary for the functioning and access of the website.

    Other cookies (such as tracking cookies or authentication cookies) are often stored for a longer period, which varies from a few days to several years. Thus, these so-called "permanent" cookies remain stored in the internet browser of the visitor's Device until a specific expiration date, unless the visitor deletes them before that deadline.

    Some cookies may impact your fundamental rights to privacy and personal data protection since they contain personal data that you, as a visitor to the Website, may disclose. For this reason, we require your consent for cookies to access or be stored on your Device. Precisely for this reason, as a company that prioritizes transparency in business and cares about your privacy, we limit the use of cookies in the manner detailed below.


  2. Who sets them?

    3. The website uses "permanent" and temporary cookies, some of which are set directly by us, but most are set by third parties. Specifically, our web server sets and stores so-called first-party cookies (first party cookies). On the other hand, third-party cookies (third party cookies) are set by third parties that provide us with support services for the proper functioning of the Website, advertising, or research for analytical purposes.


  3. Which cookies do we use?

    4. Our Website uses cookies for various purposes, but they can all be divided into the following categories of cookies based on their purpose:


    1. Analytics/Performance Cookies: These cookies are set by a third party for research purposes related to analytics. We use them to collect data on how visitors use our Website, which pages of the Website are visited most frequently, and how often, as well as how much time visitors spend on our Website. Furthermore, the third party that sets the cookie may also collect other information about you, such as your geographic location, IP address, type of Device you are using, and other information about the Device. These cookies track the performance of the Website and how it is used by visitors, all with the aim of improving its functionality. The information obtained can be used to create a unique "fingerprint" of the visitor, which will identify you on other websites you may visit and can be used to create a profile of your online activities and interests.
    2. Functionality Cookies (Preferences): We use these to allow the Website to remember the choices made by visitors (such as the language or region in which the visitor is located and other elements of the Website that the visitor can influence), as well as any changes the visitor has made regarding this, all with the aim of providing an optimal user experience.
    3. Targeting Cookies (Targeted Advertising - Marketing): We use these to collect information from the Device for the purpose of displaying targeted advertising content to the visitor that may interest them or limiting the frequency of displaying certain advertising content, as well as to help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns on the Website and other sites. They can remember that a visitor has visited a certain page of the Website and that this information is shared with third parties, including advertisers and agencies we collaborate with, and they may also be connected to the functionality of sites managed by third parties. In most cases, these cookies track visitors via the Device ID or IP address.
